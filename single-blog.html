Expert advice, practical tips, and our table navigator for you to make the right decision

Introduction

Health. Aesthetics. Cozy. The three words to describe what your clean furniture makes your home look and feel like. The eternal question our customers face sounds like this: Is it better to clean my furniture myself or call for service? We will help you solve it.

Why Furniture Cleaning
Matters At All

The problem is not only in visible dirt, dust, and pet hair. Allergens, body oils, and even bacteria — all you cannot see — still happily live on and in the fabrics. Regular cleaning helps:

  • Reduce significantly allergens and odors
  • Maintain the look and feel of your sofas, chairs, couches, and mattresses
  • Enjoy a safer home

FACT.

Indoor air can contain up to five times more pollutants and tiny particles* than the outdoor one. It happens due to closed space, absence of natural winds, and humans’ activity. Cleaning is a must for those who have children, pets, or suffer from respiratory sensitivities.

*American Lung Association’s data

Practical Tips for Cleaning Yourself

DIY has some advantages. It is painless for your budget and schedule, and can even be rather satisfying. With available household supplies, you can handle small cleaning jobs without much fuss. You do not have to pay for expert visits or extract a time slot from your schedule. You can choose your preferred cleaning agents and methods. Moreover, hands-on care helps you better understand your furniture materials. Your experience becomes richer, your expertise in furniture cleaning — vaster. If that is what you want, see below the tips from Hollywood Steamers. We also urge you to read the DIY Risks section.

Common DIY Agents and Accessories

  • White vinegar. This agent can remove odor and slightly disinfect surface.
  • Baking soda is capable to deodorize and whiten stains.
  • Mild dish soap and soft brushes or microfiber cloths. Just to wipe surface dust and deal with minor stains. This method is usually applied for gentle fabrics cleaning since prevents scratches.
  • Routine maintenance between professional cleanings.

DIY Risks

The wrong cleaner or cloth can cause fabrics damage or fade, as well as push dirt deeper into upholstery. Always check manufacturer labels and recommendations.

IMPORTANT.

Before cleaning, try every product on a tiny spot or a small piece of textile or leather on the bottom of upholstery. This way you can avoid harming the fabric of your furniture.
